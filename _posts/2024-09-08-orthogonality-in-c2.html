Welcome to my 1st profound information dump post. Below is a curated list of cool things!

Photopea
It surprises me how so many people are unaware of Photopea! This is a shockingly good Photoshop clone that can be used for quick day-to-day edits and mockups.

The Theoretical Minimum
The theoretical minimum is a book series and lecture series by Leonard Susskind which aims to provide non-academics access to physics. It's split into classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, and GR. Particularly, I'm interested in the quantum mechanics lectures. The only pre-reqs are calculus and linear algebra. Maybe do a brush up on complex vector spaces first. Here is the QM series, for free.

Spinors for Beginners (Eigenchris)
Here is a video series on spinors by Eigenchris. In the first video, at 4:43, there is a nice intuition about the orthogonality of spin up and spin down state vectors, which was something I had a hard time with, coming from working with vectors in real space.

Udio (generative music AI)
If you haven't been under a rock, you've probably played with Udio or a similar tool. However, it deserves a mention now because it supports inpainting. This makes it actually somewhat usable. Now we just need stems.
